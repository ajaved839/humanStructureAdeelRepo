Anxiety

A common feeling that can leave you stressed, afraid, or on edge. Sometimes it even shows up as physical symptoms, like a racing heart, and it can make things like hot flashes feel even more intense.

Loss of confidence and self-esteem

As with most symptoms of the menopause, loss of confidence may result from the reduction in estrogen and possibly testosterone. Many women feel like they don’t recognize themselves or they feel “a shift”. It’s difficult for them to describe but a studied phenomenon that has been noted in perimenopause and menopause and significantly affects many women’s lives.

Brain Fog

Often described as a feeling of mental cloudiness, forgetfulness, or confusion. It’s when your thinking feels slow or fuzzy—you might have trouble focusing, forget things more easily, or struggle to find the right words.

Depression

Depression can leave you feeling sad, low, and uninterested in things you usually enjoy, and it can range from mild to severe and last for different lengths of time. It’s a common part of menopause that often goes unnoticed.

+

Tinnitus

You might start noticing sounds that aren’t actually there, like ringing, buzzing, roaring, hissing, or clicking in your ears. These noises can be quiet or loud, and they might come and go or stick around.

Dental Problems

Gum disease and tooth decay can become more common during menopause. With less estrogen, you might get a dry mouth, which makes it easier for germs to stick around.

Dry skin

When estrogen drops during perimenopause and menopause, it can really change your skin. You might notice dryness, more wrinkles, acne, or extra facial hair. Skin can also get more delicate and bruise more easily.

Burning Mouth

Burning mouth syndrome: sensations like burning, heat, tingling, and pain—sometimes along with other odd feelings—even though there’s no clear sign of damage or injury. Additionally, many women notice changes to the way food tastes.

Hot flashes and night sweats

Night sweats can show up even if you don’t get daytime hot flashes. Waking up drenched, with sweaty sheets and pajamas is common.

Studies show some women experience breathing problems during the perimenopause and menopause transition. Vaginal changes

Vaginal dryness, itching, and irritation are common during menopause and affect more than a third of women in perimenopause and menopause.
